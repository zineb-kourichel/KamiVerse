What is Shinto?

◆

Shinto is Japan's indigenous spiritual tradition, rooted in the belief that the world is filled with kami — sacred spirits present in mountains, rivers, storms, ancestors, and everyday life. There is no single founder or fixed scripture; instead, Shinto lives in shrines, seasonal festivals, and quiet rituals of purification and gratitude.

Kami are not distant, all-powerful gods in the Western sense. They can be grand, like Amaterasu who lights the sky, or intimate, like the spirit of a single ancient tree. To know the kami is to understand how Japan sees the natural world — not as a backdrop, but as something alive and worthy of respect.
